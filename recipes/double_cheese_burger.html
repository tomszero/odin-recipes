Double Cheese Burger

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef (about 4 patties)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 4â€“8 slices cheddar cheese (2 per burger for double)
  • 4 burger buns
  • 2 tbsp butter (for toasting buns)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, sliced
  • Pickle slices
  • Ketchup
  • Mustard
  • Mayonnaise

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill or skillet to medium-high heat.
  2. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions and shape into patties.
  3. Season both sides of the patties with salt and pepper.
  4. Cook the patties for about 3-4 minutes on one side.
  5. Flip the patties and place a slice of cheese on each patty.
  6. Cook for another 3-4 minutes until the cheese is melted and the patties are cooked through.
  7. Stack two patties together for each burger to make it a double.
  8. Toast the burger buns lightly on the grill or skillet.
  9. Assemble the burgers by placing the double patties on the bottom bun, then add your favorite toppings (lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, sauces).
  10. Top with the other half of the bun and serve hot.
